About Me

My Journey

Holder of a Master's degree in counseling from Laval University and a member of the Order of Counselors and Career Counselors of Quebec, I have always been dedicated to helping people find meaning in their lives, regardless of the limitations or personal experiences they have faced.

My journey in counseling began with an employee assistance program, where I provided telephone support and conducted suicide risk assessments. I later worked with a diverse clientele facing multiple issues, such as mental health, addiction, prostitution, and crime, among others. With a keen interest in clients' social, physical, and professional rehabilitation, I then transitioned to a role as a probation officer before dedicating myself to the rehabilitation of Canadian veterans dealing with physical and mental health problems. For several years now, I have been offering counseling and career guidance services to a diverse clientele within Employee Assistance Programs to assist them with their personal and professional challenges.

My journey has allowed me to acquire strong skills in mental health, crisis management, and post-traumatic stress disorder. I am dedicated to supporting my clients in their challenges and leveraging my experience to provide tailored support for each situation. My goal is to help each person find sustainable solutions and restore balance in their lives.
